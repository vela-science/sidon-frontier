Evidence policy for the Sidon-set additive-combinatorics frontier.

Claims should keep theorem statements, asymptotic bounds, finite-case records,
and open problems separate. Proof artifacts and computations may support a
claim only when the source, assumptions, and scope are inspectable. Informal
problem statements are useful routing signals, but they do not settle a bound
without source-grounded review.
